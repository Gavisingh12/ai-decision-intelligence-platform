Regional agronomy update - North cluster

During the past three weeks the field teams reported a yield slowdown in the northern plots. Two conditions were repeated across reports:

1. Rainfall totals fell below the seasonal average during the flowering period.
2. Pest incidents increased after irrigation intervals were stretched to conserve water.

Operators also noted that fertilizer application stayed consistent, but soil moisture declined more sharply than expected in parcels with lighter soil composition. Market prices remained stable, so the immediate commercial pressure is operational rather than demand-driven.

Recommended agronomy actions from the report authors:
- Restore shorter irrigation intervals in high-stress plots.
- Prioritize scouting and treatment in parcels with the highest pest activity.
- Compare soil moisture trends against the last four weeks before changing fertilizer plans.
